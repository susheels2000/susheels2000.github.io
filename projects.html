Algebraic Curve Classifier

Machine learning has opened a new angle for approaching problems in pure science. As I was working on a project, I began wondering whether it could be used to understand curves and their properties. Since there is little research in this direction, I decided to start by building a curve classifier.

I worked with the relatively simple case of degree 4 curves, using a CNN-based classifier in PyTorch to distinguish between smooth, nodal, and cuspidal curves. Since nodes and cusps are not easily distinguishable at small scales, I generated curves where the tangents at a singularity had noticeable coefficients, and for nodal curves, ensured the angle between tangents was at least arctan(0.3).

Two models were trained: Max Pooling and Average Pooling. Both achieved strong results — 87.6% and 88.4% accuracy, respectively — with Average Pooling performing ~4% better on nodal curves.
